A One Day International is a form of limited overs cricket, in which each team faces a fixed number of overs, usually 50, played between two international representative teams, each having ODI status, as determined by the International Cricket Council (ICC). The first ODI was played on 5 January 1971 between Australia and England at the Melbourne Cricket Ground
